Mohammad Hatta Timeline

  • August 12, 1902: Mohammad Hatta was born in Bukittinggi, West Sumatra, then part of the Dutch East Indies.
  • 1919: Hatta went to the Netherlands for higher education in economics.
  • 1927: Hatta met Soekarno in the Netherlands, marking the beginning of their strong political partnership.
  • 1927-1932: Hatta and Soekarno established a political organization known as the Indonesian Association, which laid the groundwork for the Indonesian National Movement.
  • 1930: Hatta graduated from the University of Rotterdam with a degree in economics.
  • 1932: Returning to Indonesia, Hatta became active in the independence movement. He joined organizations like the Indonesian National Party (PNI) and founded the magazine "Pembela Tanah Air" (Defender of the Homeland).
  • 1939-1942: Hatta was imprisoned by the Dutch colonial government for his political activities.
  • August 17, 1945: Hatta, as Vice President, along with Soekarno, declared Indonesia's independence.
  • 1948-1950: Hatta served as Indonesia's first Prime Minister after independence.
  • 1950-1956: Hatta became Indonesia's first Vice President.
  • 1956-1959: Hatta served as Prime Minister again.
  • 1959-1966: Hatta served as Vice President for the second time under Sukarno.
  • 1966: Hatta resigned from the Vice Presidency following political upheavals that saw a transfer of power from Sukarno to Soeharto.
  • March 14, 1980: Mohammad Hatta passed away in Jakarta at the age of 77.
